Privacy Statement

Last updated: April 2026

The Paradox respects the privacy of everyone who visits this website or corresponds with us. This statement explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, and what rights you have. It is written to comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) and the Dutch Uitvoeringswet AVG.

1. Data controller

The data controller for personal data collected via this website is Robert Timmer, operating under the trade name The Paradox, based in The Hague, the Netherlands. For any privacy-related question, contact: robert.timmer@the-paradox.com.

2. What we collect

We only collect personal data that you actively provide to us, and only for the purpose you provide it. Specifically:

  • Inquiry form submissions: your name, role, organisation, email address, and the content of your message.
  • Direct correspondence: your email address and the content of any email you send us at robert.timmer@the-paradox.com.

This website does not use analytics cookies, tracking pixels, advertising networks, or third-party profiling tools. It does not build a profile of your browsing behaviour.

3. Why we collect it

We use the personal data you provide for one purpose only: to respond to your inquiry and, where relevant, to continue the correspondence you have initiated. We do not use your data for marketing, we do not add you to mailing lists without explicit consent, and we do not sell or share your data with third parties for commercial purposes.

4. Legal basis

Under the GDPR, the legal basis for processing this data is:

  • Your consent (Article 6(1)(a)), given by voluntarily submitting the inquiry form or sending an email.
  • Our legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f)) in responding to professional inquiries directed at us.

5. How long we keep it

Inquiry correspondence is retained for up to 24 months after our last contact, after which it is deleted unless a professional engagement is active, in which case standard commercial and tax retention periods apply (seven years under Dutch law for financial records).

6. Who processes your data

We use a small number of third-party service providers to operate the website and receive correspondence. Each operates under its own GDPR-compliant data processing terms:

  • Cloudflare, Inc. — hosting and content delivery.
  • Formspree, Inc. — inquiry form submission handling.
  • Microsoft Corporation (Microsoft 365) — email correspondence and storage.

Some of these providers are based outside the European Economic Area. Transfers are protected by Standard Contractual Clauses and equivalent safeguards.

7. Your rights

Under the GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten").
  • Object to or restrict our processing.
  • Request a portable copy of your data.
  • Withdraw consent at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, email info@the-paradox.com. We will respond within 30 days.

8. Right to lodge a complaint

If you believe your data has been handled improperly, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens): autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl.

9. Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against loss, misuse, and unauthorised access. The website uses HTTPS encryption throughout. Email correspondence is stored in a Microsoft 365 environment with standard enterprise-grade security.

10. Changes to this statement

We may update this privacy statement from time to time. Material changes will be reflected in the "Last updated" date above. We encourage you to review this statement periodically.
