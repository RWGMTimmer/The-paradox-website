Robert Timmer

Robert Timmer is an independent boardroom advisor and researcher specialising in strategy, organisational development, and human–AI collaborative decision-making.

With over two decades of experience building and leading organisations across the Netherlands, Europe, Africa, and Asia-Pacific, Robert brings a rare combination of strategic depth and operational reality to boardroom conversations. His EMBA thesis at Nyenrode Business University, completed in May 2026, produced the empirical framework on which The Paradox is built: thirteen paradoxes, eight design principles, and five novel dynamics.

Through The Paradox, he leads the governance stream: framework application, board-level diagnostics, and the advisory that shapes how the platform is deployed.

What we stand for

Operating constraints, not aspirations.

These are not positioning statements. They are the conditions under which the work is done.

01

Intellectual honesty over comfort

We distinguish between what the evidence shows and what we believe. Where the data supports a claim, we make it clearly. Where it does not, we say so.

02

Substance before visibility

We publish when we have something to say, not when we need to be seen. Every keynote, paper, and engagement is grounded in research that earns its claim.

03

Independence as a design principle

Our value comes from not being a Big Four firm, not being an AI vendor, and not taking financial positions in the organisations we advise. Independence protects the quality of the work, the integrity of the advice, and the trust of regulated clients. It is a feature, not a limitation.

04

Mutually beneficial outcomes

Achieving harmony requires a little sacrifice from all of us. We design for outcomes that serve the decision, the organisation, and the system — not just the decision-maker.

Vision

Decision quality as a designed capability.

A world where every consequential decision — in boardrooms, in institutions, in the choices individuals make that shape their lives — is made with full awareness of both human and artificial limitations. Starting with the boards that govern institutions. Scaling to the individuals those institutions serve.
