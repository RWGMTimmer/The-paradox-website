Four pillars

What the platform guarantees by design.

schema

01 / Pillar

Demonstrable decision process

Every decision follows a structured 8-phase process, from signal detection to evaluation. Considerations, alternatives, and risks are documented at every stage. Not as a compliance exercise — as the architecture through which better decisions are made.

balance

02 / Pillar

AI as challenge, not confirmation

Mandatory source referencing in every AI output. Debiasing-by-design: the platform broadens perspectives, surfaces disconfirming evidence, and challenges assumptions. Configured to widen thinking, not narrow it.

history

03 / Pillar

Traceable audit trail

Every AI contribution is linked to the decision file and remains reproducible years later. Role and source registration at the moment of decision. When a supervisor asks ‘on what basis?’ — the answer is in the system.

how_to_reg

04 / Pillar

Human decides, always

The AI agent advises and prepares. The board member decides and confirms. Human-in-the-loop is a governance requirement, not a setting to toggle.

Architecture

Three layers under the surface.

The eight design principles from the research are operationalised in the platform. Using them means running the platform, not reading a report.

The platform does not train on client data.

Layer 01

Generic sector context

Regulator guidance and sector standards, delivered through retrieval-augmented generation (RAG).

Positioning

Not a layer on top. A replacement.

Existing board portals handle agendas, document bundles, and voting — and stop there. Our platform replaces them. It carries the same agenda, document, and voting functions — now in test — and adds the part that actually determines outcomes: decision architecture, governed AI, and a reproducible audit trail of AI influence. One system, not a board portal plus a governance tool bolted on.

Delivery model

Two principals. Two roles. One decision point.

Governance stream

Robert Timmer

Leads the governance stream: diagnostics, framework application, and board engagement. The advisory shapes what the platform does.

Platform stream

Merlin IJzerman

Leads the platform stream: architecture, configuration, and technical delivery. The platform delivers what the advisory designs.

The separation mirrors the platform’s own design: human judgment and AI capability, each with a defined role. Engagements begin with a scoped pilot on a single decision process — with a defined go/no-go moment at the end. No incremental lock-in.
