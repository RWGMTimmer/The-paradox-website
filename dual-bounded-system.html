The core concept · Research

The dual-bounded system.

The dual-bounded system is the interaction, in decision-making, between human bounded rationality and the bounded learning of artificial intelligence. Where the two sets of limits meet, they do not cancel out — they compound. Understanding and governing that intersection is the central problem of human–AI decision-making.

The intersection

Two sets of limits, compounding.

Decades of research established that humans decide under bounded rationality — the cognitive limits identified by Herbert Simon. Artificial intelligence introduces a second boundary: bounded learning. It knows only what it was trained on, has no access to ground truth, and produces fluent, confident error. Put the two in the same decision and their limits overlap.

Human

Bounded rationality

Heuristics, fatigue, confirmation bias.

AI

Bounded learning

Training limits, no ground truth, fluent error.

The dual-bounded system

Why it matters

More confident.
Not more correct.

The intuitive assumption is that adding AI to a decision improves it. In a dual-bounded system that assumption fails. AI does not simply augment human judgment; it transforms it — often making decision-makers more confident without making them more correct.

The tool built to sharpen judgment can quietly blunt it. Every board is now a human–AI decision system, whether or not it has been designed as one. The competitive advantage is not AI capability. It is knowing where human and AI judgment both break down, and designing for it.

What the intersection produces

Five dynamics — nobody else has named them.

These are the mechanisms through which the dual-bounded system degrades decision quality. Each was surfaced in the original research and is countered, deliberately, by the design principles behind the platform.

01

Commitment–confirmation cycle

Early AI input anchors a position; subsequent reasoning confirms rather than challenges it.

02

Absent auto-corrective function

Neither party reliably catches the other’s errors, so mistakes propagate unchecked.

03

Noise amplification

Variability in judgment is scaled and repeated rather than dampened.

04

Two-sided capability divide

The gap between skilled and unskilled use widens on both the human and the AI side.

05

Emotional outsourcing

Responsibility and discomfort are offloaded to the machine, eroding ownership of the decision.

The research basis

Grounded, not asserted.

The dual-bounded system is the organising concept of original qualitative research completed at Nyenrode Business University in May 2026 — sixteen semi-structured interviews with executives and board members across the Netherlands, Germany, and France, and 1,080 coded quotations analysed in ATLAS.ti.

From it come thirteen paradoxes (empirical manifestations of the system), eight design principles for governing it, and the five dynamics above.
